# Online Sports Betting and Young Adults Mapped  
## LINK: https://koltonrogers15-hub.github.io/ProjectDecemberFive/
### This geospatial dashboard seeks to explore spatial patterns in the growth of online sports betting since its legalization.  

In 2018, the supreme court gave states the ability to legalize and regulate sports betting. Since that decision, 38 states have opened up to sports betting, which usually happens online. This form of recreation quickly surged among young adults, particularly young men. Media reports have described the trends in popularity of online sports betting among the college aged demographic as an epidemic. Arguments have been made that the accessibility of the online format, as well as ubiquitous sports engagement in mainstream American culture, resulted in irresponsible betting behavior in young adults across the country. My web application seeks to visualize trends in online sports betting and lend an analytical tool to spatially explore the connections between college aged population presence and online sports betting trends. 

# Explanation of visual elements  
* The upper left container presents a line graph showing the total gambling revenue (gross gaming revenue or GGR) generated from sports betting in that state. The data was dervived from the American Gaming Association reports and reports from the UNLV Center for Gaming Research.  

* The lower left container presents a bar chart showing the proportions of revenue generated from sports betting versus other forms of gambling (horse racing, retail casinos, etc). The data was dervived from the American Gaming Association reports and reports from the UNLV Center for Gaming Research.  

* The upper right container presents a bar chart showing the search trend score for online sports betting terms, such as "parlay." (Data from google trends) 

* The lower right container presents some state-specific resources for gambling safety and awareness.
